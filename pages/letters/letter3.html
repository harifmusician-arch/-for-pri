Read After We Fight ❤️

Hi Pri, If you're reading this, then there's a decent chance we're both annoyed at each other right now. Maybe we misunderstood something. Maybe one of us said something we shouldn't have. Maybe we're both sitting there waiting for the other person to text first. Who knows. What I do know is this: A bad conversation does not erase all the good ones. One argument does not erase months of memories. One rough day does not erase everything that made us care about each other in the first place. Right now, it's easy to focus on what went wrong. It's easy to replay messages. It's easy to think about who was right. Who was wrong. Who started it. Who should apologize. But when enough time passes, those things usually matter a lot less than they feel like they do in the moment. What matters is whether we still care. And if you're reading this letter, then I think we already know the answer to that. I don't expect us to agree on everything. I don't expect us to never get frustrated. I don't expect us to be perfect. I just hope that whenever we have difficult days, we remember that we're not enemies. It's me and you versus the problem. Not me versus you. So before you decide that this argument is bigger than everything else, I want you to remember a few things. Remember the conversations that made you smile. Remember the screenshots that ended up on this website. Remember the moments that felt important enough to save. Those things are real too. And they're worth remembering. Maybe by the time you're reading this, we're already okay again. Maybe we're not. Either way, I don't want one bad moment to become the only thing you see. Because when I think about you, that's not what I see. I see someone who became a very important part of my year. Someone worth understanding. Someone worth listening to. Someone worth trying again for. So let's not let one storm convince us the sky is always cloudy. We'll figure it out. We usually do. — Hari ❤️
