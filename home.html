Sometimes I think Homer, Dante, and Tolkien were all writing about the same thing. Homer called it Ithaca the place a weary traveler longs for after years of wandering. Dante called it Paradise,the light waiting beyond every dark forest. Tolkien called it the Shire,a home so ordinary and precious that its absence is only understood once it is lost. And perhaps that is why meeting you feels so impossible to explain. Not because I spent years searching for you. But because I had stopped believing I would ever find you. I had already made peace with the idea that some people are meant to keep wandering. That some stories are destined to remain unfinished. That perhaps certain kinds of happiness belonged to other people. like land on the horizon to a sailor who had long since stopped looking for shore. Like the first star seen after a night so long you forgot morning existed. Like coming home to a place I had never been before.
